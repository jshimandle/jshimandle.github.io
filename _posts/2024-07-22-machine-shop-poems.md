*Three short poems written during a semester of fabrication lab.*

---

**Tolerance**

Everything fits
within a range.
The engineers call this
acceptable deviation.

I've been trying to learn
the same grace
for the things I build
of less certain materials.

---

**Chip Formation**

The tool removes
what the designer decided
was unnecessary.

The chips fall,
curl,
and are swept away—
this is not waste,
it is the part
becoming itself.

---

**Heat Treatment**

Stress relieved at 600°,
quenched,
tempered back to useful hardness.

The metallurgist says
the grain structure refines.
The atoms find
more stable arrangements.

I think about this
when I need to be
somewhere very cold
then very hot
then cooled slowly
to whatever I am
supposed to be.

---

*These poems appeared first in a notebook kept in the machine shop during spring semester. They smelled slightly of cutting oil when I transcribed them.*
