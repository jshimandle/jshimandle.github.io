The sun rises so joyfully behind you.\
The day passes with a turn of your head,\
And the moonlight hits your face softly\
Like the snow on your hair—\
Delicate and pure.

I love your hair, bristly and pine;\
Tall and strong, it builds my home\
And warms me through cold winters.\
I love the breath you breathe.\
I surely live by it.

Your eyes are an ocean,\
Your dimples, lakes.\
I want to drink from your rivers\
And feel their power tumbling through\
Well designed turbines.

Your heart is rich with passion, burning fierce.\
It drives me on, as it does for many men.\
It is a diamond’s younger child.\
But we love it more, for it burns brighter,\
And blackens the lungs of my brothers with its soot.

I love your blood,\
Black with the bones of my ancestors.\
It bathes my children in the wealth\
That only blood can buy—\
Dripping with power and deployable energy.

You are deep blues and fertile browns.\
But do not worry, I will wash you with green.\
Then I will dry you up.\
Sleep deeply tonight.\
Sunsets are so beautiful.

---

*A love poem I wrote for my poetry workshop class in the spring of 2026.*

*To the earth from an oil baron.*
