## Overview & Motivation
I've been working as an undergraduate researcher with MIT BioInstrumentation Laboratory since 2023 as part of a long running effort to develop methane conversion technology to curb global emissions. In this specific project I designed and fabricated the sealed sensor housing, wiring, and software for an integrated gas analyzer in order to support the characterization process for certain catalysts. 


## Objectives
Design and fabricate a quick response gas analyzer capable of measuring levels of CH4, CO2, H20, CO, H2, and N2 to a high level of accuracy and temporal detail, to be used in the testing and implementation of a methane conversion device targeting emissions from cattle.

## Contributions
Designed an SLA 3D-printed housing to hold 5 sensors in series along the path of air flow while maintaining a tight seal. Wired all sensors into the same microcontroller system and developed a python script to allow for effective processing of sensor readings in unison.

## Outcomes
Complete sensor was successfully integrated into testing systems and operated consistently under test conditions.
