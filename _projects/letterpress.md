## About This Project

Completed during a book arts workshop, these broadsides feature three of my original poems
set in hand-picked Caslon metal type and printed on dampened Rives BFK paper.

The constraint of working within a fixed type cabinet — only what characters exist — forced
a kind of editorial discipline rarely present in digital typesetting. Line breaks became
structural decisions. Every em dash had to be earned.

## Process

Type was hand-set in a composing stick, locked up in a chase with furniture and quoins,
and proofed on newsprint before final printing. Ink mixing required about 45 minutes of
adjustment to achieve consistent coverage across the 12" x 18" bed.

Binding was a simple Japanese stab-stitch through a folded signature, using linen thread
and a bone folder for sharp creases.

## Reflection

There's something irreplaceable about a process that demands you slow down. The tactility
of lead type, the resistance of the press, the smell of oil-based ink — none of it is
efficient, all of it is intentional.
